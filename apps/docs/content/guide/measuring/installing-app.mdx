The production mobile release is distributed for **Android** through Google Play,
published publicly as **openJII** by **OpenJII** (rated PEGI 3; the listing notes
that the app shares location).

<Callout type="info">
  There is no published iOS app. The measurement devices used today connect over USB serial or
  Bluetooth Classic, and neither channel is available to an app on iOS, so the field app ships on
  Android only.
</Callout>

1. Open the [openJII listing on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.openjii.app).
2. Install or update **openJII**.
3. Open the app while connected to the internet.
4. Sign in with email, GitHub, or ORCID.

![The openJII Android home screen, signed into the dev testing environment](/img/mobile/app-home.png)

_Captured with production app 2.16.1 (build 160). The yellow **DEV** badge
identifies the required dev environment._

## Sign in by email

Enter the email address on your openJII account. The app sends a six-digit code;
you can read that code on another device and type it into the field phone. No
password is required.

GitHub and ORCID sign-in leave the app briefly and return through the `openjii`
app link after authentication.

## Prepare offline access

Your first sign-in requires connectivity. After sign-in, the app caches your
member experiments and each experiment's pinned workbook version. Before going
offline:

- wait until the experiment shows **Offline ready**;
- connect and test the device;
- confirm that the experiment has a workbook and protocol;
- keep the app signed in.

If an experiment or workbook changes later, reconnect and open the app so it can
refresh the cache. See [Working offline & syncing](/guide/measuring/offline-sync).

<Callout type="warn">
  Do not rely on an old minimum-device specification copied from earlier docs. Check the current
  Google Play compatibility message for the actual field phone.
</Callout>
