openJII uses Databricks in its ingestion and processing architecture. Researchers
with advanced work may also collaborate through notebooks, but the current web
interface has no self-service **Open notebook** or **Request Databricks access**
control.

Start with a normal [dataset export](/guide/data-analysis/exporting-data) when the
work fits in local R, Python, Julia, or a statistics package. That route is
portable and keeps the analysis environment under your control.

## When a notebook collaboration may help

- the dataset is too large for a practical local export;
- analysis needs raw or intermediate pipeline data not exposed as a convenient
  table;
- a team needs a shared, reproducible compute environment;
- platform engineers must add or validate a reusable transformation.

Before asking for help, prepare the experiment ID, dataset/table name, scientific
question, required columns, expected output, collaborators, and any data-access
constraints. Send that context through [Getting help](/guide/reference/getting-help).

## The medallion terms

- **Bronze** preserves source records close to their ingested form.
- **Silver** validates and standardizes data for reliable reuse.
- **Gold** presents analysis-oriented tables or aggregates.

These names describe pipeline responsibilities, not a guarantee that every
researcher can browse each internal layer. For the technical design, see
[Architecture & Pipeline](/developers/architecture) and
[Medallion layers](/developers/architecture/medallion-layers).

<Callout type="warn">
  Never place credentials, private device keys, or data outside your approved access scope in a
  notebook or support request.
</Callout>
