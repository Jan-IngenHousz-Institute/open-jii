You need one openJII account to use the web platform and the mobile app. There is
no separate sign-up: the same screen handles logging in and creating a new account.

## Ways to sign in

On the **Log in or sign up** screen you have three options:

- **Email** — we send a 6-digit code to your email. No password to remember.
- **GitHub** — sign in with your GitHub account.
- **ORCID** — sign in with your ORCID account, so your work stays linked to your
  academic identity.

![The openJII Log in or sign up screen with an email field and GitHub and ORCID options.](/img/guide/web/login-signup.png)

<Callout type="info">
  Email, GitHub, and ORCID establish your account. After the first sign-in, you can add a passkey as
  another way to open that same account.
</Callout>

## Sign up with an email code

<Steps>
<Step>
### Open the sign-in screen

Go to the openJII web platform and select **Log in or sign up**.

</Step>

<Step>
### Enter your email

Type your email address and select **Continue with Email**. We send a 6-digit
code to that address.

</Step>

<Step>
### Enter the code

Open the email, copy the 6-digit code, and enter it on the screen. If the code
does not arrive within a minute, you can request a new one.

</Step>

<Step>
### Complete your profile

The first time you sign in, we ask for a few details to finish creating your
account:

- **First Name**
- **Last Name**
- Accept the **terms and conditions**

Select **Register** to finish. After that you go straight to your dashboard each
time you sign in.

</Step>
</Steps>

## Sign up with GitHub or ORCID

Select **Continue with GitHub** or **Continue with ORCID** and approve the sign-in
with that provider. The first time, you may still be asked to complete your
profile (first and last name).

If your provider does not share an email address with openJII (this can happen
with ORCID), we ask you to add one so features like project transfers can reach
you.

## One account, web and mobile

Your openJII account works on both the web platform and the
[mobile app](/guide/measuring). Sign in to the app with the same option you use on
the web.

<Callout type="info">
  Signing in to the mobile app by email? You enter your email in the app, then read the 6-digit code
  from your inbox on any device and type it into the app. You do not need your email inbox on the
  phone itself.
</Callout>
