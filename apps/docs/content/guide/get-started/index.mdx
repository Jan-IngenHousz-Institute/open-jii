openJII is an open platform for plant-science research. It helps you collect
photosynthesis and plant measurements from the field, bring them together in one
place, analyze them, and share them with the wider research community.

It is built and run by the [Jan IngenHousz Institute](https://www.jan-ingenhousz-institute.org),
a non-profit research institute focused on photosynthesis, and it is open source.

## Who it is for

openJII is built for the people doing plant research in the real world:

- Plant scientists and agricultural researchers taking field measurements
- Research groups running experiments across sites and seasons
- Universities and courses teaching plant physiology with real data
- Crop-breeding programs comparing many plant lines

You do not need to be a programmer to use it. The web platform and the mobile app
are designed for researchers first.

## How it works

Everything in openJII follows the same simple arc: **measure, analyze, share**.

<Cards>
  <Card title="Measure">
    Take measurements in the field with the openJII mobile app and a handheld device such as the
    MultispeQ, or stream data continuously from IoT sensors. The mobile app works offline and syncs
    when you are back online.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Analyze">
    Your measurements collect in one place, organized by experiment. View trends, filter, and
    compare your data in the web platform, and export it for your own analysis.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Share">
    Invite collaborators to your experiment, and choose when your data becomes public. openJII is
    built around open, FAIR data.
  </Card>
</Cards>
