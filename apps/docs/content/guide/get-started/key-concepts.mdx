A few words come up again and again in openJII. Here is what each one means, in
plain language. Each concept links to a fuller page.

## Experiment

An **experiment** is the main way you organize your work. It brings together your
measurements, the people you work with, and the settings for a single study,
usually one plant species or one research question.

Everything you collect belongs to an experiment. You can have as many as you need.

[Working with experiments →](/guide/experiments)

## Protocol

A **protocol** is the set of instructions a device follows to take a measurement,
the measurement steps, timing, and settings. openJII comes with ready-made
protocols, and you can pick the one that fits your study.

Some protocol settings are adjustable within your experiment. Changing them only
affects your experiment, never the original protocol.

[Choosing and adjusting protocols →](/guide/devices-protocols)

## Device vs Sensor

openJII makes a small but useful distinction:

<Cards>
  <Card title="Device">
    Hardware you operate by hand, one measurement at a time, guided by a protocol. The **MultispeQ**
    handheld is the main example. You use it with the mobile app in the field.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sensor">
    Hardware that runs on its own and streams data continuously with little day-to-day input, such
    as an Ambyte IoT sensor set up in a field.
  </Card>
</Cards>

In short: you _take_ measurements with a device; a sensor _keeps_ measuring on its
own.

[Devices and sensors →](/guide/devices-protocols)

## Roles

Within an experiment, each person has one of two roles:

- **Admin** — sets up and owns the experiment; can change its settings, manage
  members, and invite others.
- **Member** — contributes measurements and comments, typically the person out in
  the field with the mobile app.

[Collaborators and roles →](/guide/experiments/collaborators)

## Visibility and embargo

openJII is built around open data, so experiments are **public by default**. You
can instead keep an experiment **private** for a while by setting an **embargo**,
a date on which it becomes public automatically. The embargo can run up to about
a year.

[Sharing, visibility, and embargo →](/guide/sharing)
