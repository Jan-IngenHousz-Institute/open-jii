This is the shortest path from a brand-new account to your first measurement. You
create an experiment on the web, take a measurement in the field with the mobile
app, and watch it show up in your data.

{/* NEEDS-LIVE-CHECK: run this end to end on a new account + current mobile build + real MultispeQ to confirm the measured duration and that these are the minimum steps. */}
You can expect it to take around ten minutes.

## Before you start

You will need:

- An **openJII account** (see [Create your account](/guide/get-started/create-account)).
- An **Android phone** (Android 7.0 or newer, at least 2 GB of RAM).
- A **MultispeQ** handheld device.

<PlatformCta path="/en-US/platform/experiments/new">Create your first experiment</PlatformCta>

## Step by step

<Steps>
<Step>
### Create an experiment

On the web platform, open **Experiments** and select **Create Experiment**. Give
it a **Name**, and add a short **Description** if you like.

{/* verified-live: dev.openjii.org 2026-07-20 — the authorized capture account had no selectable workbooks in the create wizard, and creation succeeded with Workbook: None. */}
The workbook is optional for creating the experiment shell. In the observed dev
account, the picker offered only **None**. Step through the wizard and select
**Create Experiment**.

<video controls width="100%" preload="metadata">
  <source src="/img/guide/web/quick-start-web.webm" type="video/webm" />
  Your browser does not support embedded video.
</video>

<Callout type="warn">
  An experiment created with **Workbook: None** is not ready for protocol-guided measurement. Before
  continuing on mobile, an admin must attach a suitable workbook containing the intended protocol.
  If the workbook picker is empty, ask the workbook owner or platform team to make the correct
  published workbook available to your account.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step>
### Install the mobile app and sign in

On your Android phone, install the **openJII** app from the Google Play Store.
Open it and sign in with the **same account** you use on the web.

{/* NEEDS-LIVE-CHECK: confirm current app store listing/name and the sign-in screen on the shipping build. */}

</Step>

<Step>
### Connect your MultispeQ

Turn on your MultispeQ and connect it to the app over **Bluetooth**.

![The Devices sheet before a MultispeQ is paired](/img/mobile/device-pairing.png)

The current app opens a **Devices** sheet with **Scan** and an
**Allow Bluetooth scanning** action. With no instrument connected it also
prompts you to hold the MultispeQ button for three seconds.

{/* NEEDS-LIVE-CHECK: confirm the exact pairing flow and screen labels on the current mobile build. */}
{/* MEDIA-PARTIAL: the setup state is captured; a connected-device still requires a physical MultispeQ. */}

</Step>

<Step>
### Take a measurement

Pick your experiment from the list, then follow the on-screen steps. The app
guides you through the protocol, answering any questions and taking the reading.
When it finishes, you see a preview of the result.

{/* NEEDS-LIVE-CHECK: confirm the measurement flow screens and that a single measurement completes as described on the current build. */}
{/* MEDIA-WAIVER: no physical MultispeQ was connected during capture, so a protocol-guided hardware result was not simulated. The questions-only production-app excerpt lives on Taking measurements in the field. */}

</Step>

<Step>
### Sync and view your data

Upload the measurement from the app. If you are offline, the app stores it and
syncs automatically once you are back online.

Back on the web platform, open your experiment and go to the **Data** tab. Your
measurement appears there.

{/* NEEDS-LIVE-CHECK: confirm how soon a synced measurement appears in the web Data tab (processing interval). */}
![A populated experiment Data tab after measurements have been processed](/img/guide/web/data-first-measurement.png)

</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  The mobile app is **offline-first**: after you sign in, your experiments are cached so you can
  measure with no connectivity and sync later.
</Callout>
