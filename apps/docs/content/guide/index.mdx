Welcome to the openJII researcher guide. openJII is an open platform for
plant-science research: collect photosynthesis and plant measurements in the
field, bring them together in one place, analyze them, and share them with the
wider research community.

It is built and run by the
[Jan IngenHousz Institute](https://www.jan-ingenhousz-institute.org), a
non-profit research institute focused on photosynthesis, and it is open source.
You do not need to be a programmer to use it: the web platform and the mobile
app are designed for researchers first.

## The measure, analyze, share arc

Everything in openJII follows the same simple arc:

<Cards>
  <Card title="Measure" href="/guide/measuring">
    Take handheld MultispeQ readings with the offline-first mobile app, or ingest continuous data
    from Ambyte sensors.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Analyze" href="/guide/data-analysis">
    Your measurements land in searchable tables with built-in charts, exports, and macro-based
    analysis.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Share" href="/guide/sharing">
    Invite collaborators, keep data private under embargo, and publish it openly when you are ready.
  </Card>
</Cards>
