You create an experiment through a short guided wizard. Only a name is required,
so you can start simple and fill in the rest later.

To begin, open **Experiments** and select **Create Experiment**.

![The experiments list with the Create Experiment button](/img/guide/web/create-experiment-entry.png)

<PlatformCta path="/en-US/platform/experiments/new">Create an experiment in openJII</PlatformCta>

## The create wizard

The wizard has four steps. You can move **Next** and **Back** between them, and
nothing is created until the final step.

<Steps>
<Step>
### Details

- **Name** _(required)_ — a clear, descriptive name makes your experiment easy to
  find and share.
- **Description** _(optional)_ — a short summary of the experiment's purpose.
- **Workbook** _(optional)_ — the measurement flow the mobile app will follow.
  You can attach one now or add it later.

</Step>

<Step>
### Members & Visibility

- **Add Members** — search for people to add now, and give each the **Member** or
  **Admin** role. You can also do this later.
- **Visibility** — choose **Public** or **Private**. New experiments are **public
  by default**. If you choose Private, set an **embargo** date on which the
  experiment becomes public automatically.

<Callout type="warn">
  Once an experiment is public, its visibility cannot be changed back to private.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Locations

Add one or more geographic **locations** where the experiment takes place. This is
optional.

</Step>

<Step>
### Review

Check your settings, then select **Create Experiment**. Your new experiment opens
and appears in your experiments list.

</Step>
</Steps>

![The Details step in the four-step create-experiment wizard](/img/guide/web/create-experiment-details.png)

## About visibility and embargo

openJII is built around open data:

- **Public** experiments are visible to everyone. This is the default.
- **Private** experiments stay hidden until their **embargo** date, then become
  public automatically. The embargo can run up to about a year.

You can read more in [Sharing & Collaboration](/guide/sharing).

## What comes next

- The Details step lets you attach a **Workbook** (measurement flow). Set up what

- [Invite collaborators](/guide/experiments/collaborators) and set their roles.
