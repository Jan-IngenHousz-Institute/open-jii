Experiments are meant to be shared. You can invite collaborators, give each of
them a role, and manage everyone from one place.

## The two roles

Within an experiment, each person has one of two roles:

- **Admin** — sets up and owns the experiment. Admins can change its settings,
  manage collaborators, and do everything a member can.
- **Member** — contributes to the experiment: takes measurements with the mobile
  app, and adds comments. This is usually the person in the field.

An experiment can have several admins. Public experiments can be viewed by anyone,
even people who are not members.

### What each role can do

| Action                                                   | Member | Admin |
| :------------------------------------------------------- | :----: | :---: |
| See the experiment, its data and visualizations          |   ✓    |   ✓   |
| Take measurements with the mobile app                    |   ✓    |   ✓   |
| Add comments and flags                                   |   ✓    |   ✓   |
| Edit experiment settings (name, description, visibility) |   –    |   ✓   |
| Upload data and metadata on the web                      |   –    |   ✓   |
| Create and edit visualizations                           |   –    |   ✓   |
| Add or remove collaborators and change their roles       |   –    |   ✓   |
| Archive the experiment                                   |   –    |   ✓   |

## Invite a collaborator

Open your experiment and go to the **Collaborators** tab, then select **Invite**.
There are two ways to add someone, both from the same search field:

- **Add an existing user** — search by name or email and select them. They get
  access immediately with the role you choose.
- **Invite someone by email** — enter an email address that is not yet on openJII
  and choose **Send invite by email**. They receive an email, and they join the
  experiment automatically once they create an account.

Choose the role (**Member** or **Admin**) as you invite. New collaborators are
members by default.

The Collaborators tab groups people into three sub-tabs:

- **Members** — people who already have access.
- **Invited** — pending email invitations. Admins can **Revoke** one before it is
  accepted.
- **Requests** — pending requests to join (see below).

## Requests to join a public experiment

Anyone viewing a **public** experiment they are not part of can select **Request
to join**, with an optional message. Admins see these under the **Requests**
sub-tab and can **Approve** or **Reject** them. Approved people join as members.

## Changing roles and removing people

Admins can change a collaborator's role or remove them from the **Members**
sub-tab. A member can also remove themselves with **Leave**.

<Callout type="info">
  An experiment always needs at least one admin. You cannot remove or demote the last admin.
</Callout>
