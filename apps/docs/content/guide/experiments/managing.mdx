The **Experiments** page is your home base. It lists your experiments and gives
you a few ways to find the one you want.

![The experiments list with search, filtering, and representative experiment cards](/img/guide/web/experiments-list.png)

## Find an experiment

Each experiment appears as a card showing its **name**, **description**, and when
it was **last updated**. Select a card to open the experiment.

Two controls help you narrow the list:

- **Search** — type in the search box to match experiments by name and
  description (and by the people and locations linked to them).
- **Filter** — switch between:
  - **Member Experiments** — experiments you are a member of.
  - **All Experiments** — your experiments plus public experiments from others.

## Create and transfer

From the top of the Experiments page you can also:

- **Create Experiment** — start a new experiment (see
  [Creating an experiment](/guide/experiments/creating)).
- **Request Project Transfer** — bring in an experiment, its protocols, and its
  data from another platform.

## Archiving

When you are finished with an experiment, you can **archive** it. Archived
experiments move out of the main list into a separate view, which you reach with
**View Archived Experiments**. This keeps your working list focused without losing
anything.
